# Interview Playbook for New Grads

A complete, role-agnostic guide for landing your first job out of school. Read in order on your first pass — each document builds on the previous one. After that, return to specific sections as you progress through the funnel.

## How the funnel actually works

```
Resume submitted ─► Recruiter screen ─► Hiring manager screen ─► Loop / panel ─► Team match ─► Offer
```

Resume-to-callback rates for cold applications are typically 1–5% for new grads at competitive companies — a small fraction of resumes get a recruiter conversation, and a small fraction of those convert to a final offer. Referrals materially improve the resume-to-callback rate (anecdotally several times higher), which is why doc 03 matters so much. Treat any specific conversion number you see online as directional, not a fact — they vary wildly by company, role, and timing.

## How to use this playbook

- **Two weeks out from your first interview:** read the whole thing once.
- **Day before each interview:** re-read the relevant doc + the questions-to-ask doc.
- **After every interview:** journal what was asked, what you wish you'd said, what to add to your story bank.

## What "shining" actually looks like

Interviewers compare you to a bar, not to other candidates in the moment. To clear the bar, you need four things visible in roughly this order:

1. **Clarity** — they understand what you said the first time.
2. **Specificity** — your stories have numbers, names, and outcomes, not vibes.
3. **Self-awareness** — you can name what you did wrong and what you'd change.
4. **Drive** — they believe you'll figure things out when no one tells you what to do.

Almost everything in this playbook is a tactic for making one of those four things show up on demand.
