# 11 — Common Pitfalls (One-Page Cheat Sheet)

Read this the night before any interview, on top of the relevant deep-dive doc. Each line is a failure mode that has cost real candidates real offers. The fuller treatment lives in the doc referenced.

## Strategy (00, 03)

- Spraying applications instead of targeting + referring → low conversion.
- Skipping networking because it feels icky → leaves the highest-ROI lever on the floor.
- Starting too late for your industry's recruiting cycle → see doc 13 for timing.
- Refusing tier-3 / safe-tier companies → no interview reps before your dream loops.
- Treating the search as a side task → it's a 6–9 month project.

## Resume + application (01)

- "We" everywhere → no signal about what you did.
- Bullets list responsibilities, not outcomes.
- Buried best bullet — the strongest one should be first under each role.
- 30-skill stuffing → noise, hard to defend in interviews.
- Same resume to every role → no tailoring, no LLM-screener match.
- Typos, especially in the company name → auto-rejection.
- Two pages → "I can't prioritize."

## Online presence (02, 12)

- Unprofessional photo, sunglasses, group crop.
- Empty GitHub or one repo of homework.
- Public socials full of liabilities → lock or clean.
- Personal email like `xXskaterboiXx@…` → use `firstname.lastname`.
- Custom LinkedIn URL not set.

## Pre-interview

- Didn't research the company → "what does your company do?" is fatal.
- Didn't look up the interviewer on LinkedIn → 5 minutes of free signal lost.
- Showed up tired → sleep beats one more practice problem.
- Didn't test the platform → mid-interview login failure reads as careless.
- For async video (12): no test recording, bad lighting, virtual background glitching.

## Behavioral (04)

- "We" instead of "I" once you reach the action phase.
- Drowning in the situation, never reaching the action.
- Fake failure ("I worked too hard") or real failure with no learning.
- Conflict story where you were 100% right and they were 100% wrong.
- Memorized "tell me about yourself" recited without eye contact.
- No questions back at the end (08).

## Technical (05)

- Diving into code before clarifying the problem → solving the wrong thing perfectly.
- Going silent → interviewer can't grade what they can't hear.
- Refusing hints → they're trying to help you succeed.
- Forgetting to verify → "I think it works" without testing.
- Using a language you sort of know to seem advanced.

## Case (06)

- Reciting a memorized framework instead of listening first.
- Burning 10 minutes on one branch.
- Forgetting to recommend.
- Hidden mental arithmetic → math errors no one can catch.

## Communication (07)

- Filler words every 5 seconds.
- Speeding up as you get less sure (do the opposite).
- Talking over interviewer silence (they're often taking notes).
- Negative phrasing — "I haven't really done that" → "I've focused on Y; the closest analog is Z."

## Post-interview + offer (09, 10)

- No thank-you note.
- Same thank-you with the wrong name pasted.
- Daily anxiety follow-ups → desperate.
- Lying about competing offers → catastrophic when found out.
- Accepting on the call without reviewing.
- Skipping negotiation → most expensive 30 minutes you'll ever skip.
- Comparing base-to-base instead of total comp.
- Reneging after acceptance → industry is small.

## AI use (12)

- Letting LLM write your cover letter from scratch → recruiters detect the voice.
- Mass-applying via auto-applier → blacklisted at many companies.
- Violating an explicit no-AI policy on take-homes → exposed in follow-up walkthroughs.
- Reading from a hidden screen during live interviews → detectable, often disqualifying.

## Mindset (00)

- Tying self-worth to outcomes.
- Comparing your funnel to LinkedIn announcements → survivorship bias.
- Going dark when discouraged → momentum is the search's most valuable asset.

## The single biggest pitfall

Believing you're behind because someone else announced an offer in October. Different industries hire on different cycles (see doc 13). Run your race.
